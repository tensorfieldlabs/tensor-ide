Spring

The frost retreats without a sound,
And green reclaims the hardened ground.
A thousand buds begin to swell,
With quiet stories left to tell.

The robin finds its voice again,
A thread of song through morning rain.
The creek runs high, the willows bend,
As winter draws its bitter end.

Light lingers longer every day,
The world shakes off its cold dismay.
Blossoms break through bark and stone,
A reminder — nothing stays alone.

Spring does not arrive with fanfare loud,
But slips beneath a thinning cloud.
A slow return, a steady climb,
The earth keeping its ancient time.
